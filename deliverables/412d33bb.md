## Section 6: FAQs — New Hire Team Building

**Q: What is new hire team building?**
New hire team building refers to structured group activities and facilitated experiences designed specifically for newly joined employees — with the goal of accelerating belonging, building cross-functional relationships, communicating company culture, and reducing first-year attrition. It differs from general team building in its specific focus on the integration of new cohorts.

**Q: When is the best time to run a new hire team building program?**
Ideally within the first 30–60 days of employment, when new hires are most receptive to culture signals and relationship formation. Many companies align new hire team building with quarterly onboarding cohort cycles or with the start of the fiscal year.

**Q: How large or small can a new hire team building group be with UPE?**
UPE produces new hire programs for groups ranging from 20 to 500+ participants. We calibrate the program design, venue selection, and facilitation format to the specific group size and composition.

**Q: What makes UPE's new hire team building different from a standard activity provider?**
UPE is a full-service corporate event production company, not an activity vendor. We design the entire program — narrative, logistics, activities, content, facilitation, catering, entertainment, and post-program reporting — as one coherent experience aligned with your HR and culture objectives.

**Q: Can UPE run new hire team building outside Spain?**
Yes. UPE produces events across 130+ global destinations. Spain is our production hub, but we can design and execute your new hire program wherever it makes strategic sense — from Tel Aviv to Amsterdam to Lisbon.

**Q: How do we measure the ROI of a new hire team building program?**
UPE builds measurement into every program. We track participant engagement scores, cohort retention data (in partnership with your HR team), and qualitative feedback. The primary ROI metric is first-year attrition reduction — even a 5% improvement in new hire retention generates measurable savings that far exceed program cost.
