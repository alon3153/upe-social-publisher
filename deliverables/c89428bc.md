### H2: Why Team Building for New Hires Is Different

Generic team building and new hire team building share some superficial similarities — both involve groups of people doing activities together. But the **objective, the emotional context, and the success criteria** are entirely different.

In a new hire team building event:
- Participants do **not yet trust each other** — they are strangers at various stages of personal vulnerability
- Some attendees may feel **imposter syndrome or anxiety** about being new, which generic competitive activities can amplify rather than relieve
- The goal is not to identify who is a strong performer — it is to **create psychological safety and shared identity**
- Company values and culture messaging must be **woven into the experience** organically, not presented as a slide deck
- The event must be **accessible and inclusive** for people from different countries, seniority levels, backgrounds, and physical abilities

UPE's new hire programs are designed around these realities. Every activity choice, every facilitation moment, and every production element is selected to build **belonging first, performance second**.
