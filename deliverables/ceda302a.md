## ✍️ פוסט 2 | Creative Director | יום שלישי | אנגלית
**סוג:** Practical Framework | נושא: Creative brief anatomy

---

**[טקסט מוכן לתזמון]**

Most corporate event briefs I receive look like this:

- 200 attendees
- 2-day conference
- "We want it to be engaging and memorable"
- Budget: [redacted]
- Deadline: 6 weeks

After 15 years in event production, I've learned to ask 5 questions that no brief ever includes — but that change everything about what we build.

**The 5 questions that actually shape a great event:**

**→ What conversation should happen in the car ride home?**
Not the headline message. The one sentence a senior manager says to their team on Monday morning.

**→ Who in the room is skeptical?**
Every corporate event has someone who came against their will. Design for them. When you win the skeptics, you win the room.

**→ What's the bravest thing this organization could do in a room together?**
Not the safest. The bravest. The answer reveals what the brand actually believes vs. what it says.

**→ What would make the CEO uncomfortable?**
Good events create productive tension. If nothing is slightly uncomfortable, nothing will be remembered.

**→ What's the story the brand is NOT telling yet?**
Internal pride is underused. The most powerful conference moments often spotlight the people, not the product.

These 5 questions take 20 minutes. They save months of mediocre creative development.

Save this — and if you're planning an event for 2026/2027, try asking your agency these questions before they present the first concept.

**[טקסט מוכן לתזמון]**

There are 3 levels of corporate event creative. Most programs stop at Level 1.

**Level 1: Functional Creative**
→ Logo on the stage. Theme colors on the backdrop. Opening video with the CEO.
→ It works. It doesn't embarrass anyone. Nobody talks about it next week.

**Level 2: Atmospheric Creative**
→ Immersive entrance experience. Thoughtful lighting design. Set pieces that reflect the brand story.
→ People notice. They take photos. A few post on Instagram.
→ This is where most "premium" events live.

**Level 3: Transformative Creative**
→ The experience changes how participants see their role in the company.
→ They leave with a different internal narrative.
→ Six months later, leaders still reference what happened in that room.

The difference between Level 2 and Level 3 isn't budget.

It's a question asked before any design begins:
**"What do we want people to *believe* that they don't believe yet?"**
