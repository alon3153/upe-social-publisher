MICE can no longer be treated as a discretionary spend to be trimmed when times get tough. In an increasingly fragmented world, meetings, incentives, and conferences are becoming strategic tools that drive revenue and culture.

The MICE industry in 2026 is defined by five strategic shifts: Purpose-Driven Incentives that align travel with CSR goals, strict ESG compliance with measurable carbon reporting, the rise of secondary destination hubs to counter over-tourism, digital-detox and wellness-first retreats designed for deeper engagement, and hyper-personalized itineraries powered by data and predictive insights.

88% of corporate travelers prefer eco-conscious event partners. If your production company can't tell you their carbon-per-delegate metric, that's a problem your procurement team will flag.

The market for mid-sized events (150-249 participants) is growing, as organizations increasingly prefer this meeting size for strategy sessions, regional conferences, and product launches — it balances scale with personalization while remaining cost-effective.

For European companies: Seville over Madrid. Porto over Lisbon. Thessaloniki over Athens. Same magic, better pricing, zero over-tourism pressure.
