Half our pipeline was invisible.

1. LinkedIn (organic / paid)
2. Inbound (website / SEO)
3. Referral (client / partner)
4. Outbound (sales-initiated)
5. Event / Expo

Companies that consistently track lead sources report 2x better pipeline visibility — and that visibility is the difference between a marketing strategy and a marketing guess.

**[Variant B — The Budget Decision Hook]**

You can't optimize what you can't measure.

And you can't measure what isn't tagged.

At UPE — 1,500+ events produced, 130+ destinations, 25,000+ participants — we found ourselves in an uncomfortable position: nearly half our Salesforce Opportunities had no Lead Source. Not "Other." Not "Unknown." Just blank.

Attribution isn't an analytics project — it's a budget allocation project. Every month you rely on incomplete attribution, you're systematically over-investing in "deal closer" channels and under-investing in the awareness channels that create purchase intent.

So we fixed it. Hard stop. Three steps:

**1. Backfill** — Manually traced and tagged every open Opportunity with a missing source
**2. Standardize** — Locked five canonical Lead Source values in Salesforce (restricted picklist, no free text)
**3. Gate** — Made Lead Source a mandatory field before any Opportunity moves to Closed

The result: our ROI analysis finally means something.

Lock down Lead Source as a restricted picklist. Train your sales and marketing teams to use the standard values. That's it. That's the whole playbook.

### 🔒 כלל ה-Gate — Opportunity לא נסגרת ללא Source

**הגדרה טכנית ב-Salesforce:**
- שדה `Lead_Source__c` / `LeadSource` → **Required field** על Page Layout של Opportunity
- Validation Rule: אם `StageName = 'Closed Won' OR 'Closed Lost'` AND `LeadSource = NULL` → שגיאה עם הודעה: *"חובה למלא Lead Source לפני סגירת ה-Opportunity"*

```
AND(
  OR(StageName = "Closed Won", StageName = "Closed Lost"),
  ISBLANK(LeadSource)
)
```

**Error Message:** `"שדה Lead Source חובה לפני מעבר ל-Closed. בחר אחד מ-5 הערכים הסטנדרטיים."`
